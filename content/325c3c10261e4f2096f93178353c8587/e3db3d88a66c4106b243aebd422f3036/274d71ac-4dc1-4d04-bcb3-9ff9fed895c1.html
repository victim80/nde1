An IDS is also classified as a centralized IDS or a distributed IDS, this classification is based on the structure of the IDS

Distributed Structure of an IDS

A distributed intrusion detection system (dIDS) consists of multiple IDSs over a large network. These systems communicate with each other or with a central server that facilitates an advanced network of monitoring, incident analysis, and instant attack data. By having these cooperative agents distributed across a network, network operators can get a broader view of what is occurring on their network as a whole.

dIDS also allows a company to efficiently manage its incident analysis resources by centralizing its attack records and by giving the analyst a way to spot new trends or patterns and identify threats to the network across multiple network segments.
